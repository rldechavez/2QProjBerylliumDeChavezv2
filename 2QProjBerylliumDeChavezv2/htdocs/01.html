Painting / Drawing

What is Painting?

Painting, the expression of ideas and emotions, with the creation of certain aesthetic qualities, in a two-dimensional visual language. The elements of this language - its shapes, lines, colours, tones, and textures - are used in various ways to produce sensations of volume, space, movement, and light on a flat surface. These elements are combined into expressive patterns in order to represent real or supernatural phenomena, to interpret a narrative theme, or to create wholly abstract visual relationships.

There are different styles or depictions under the category or form Painting. The list below is some of the most known techniques or styles in painting.

Realism (also known as naturalism)
- is a style of art that where the artist attempts to portray the subject in realistically but not appearing as a photograph. In this style of art, the artist doesn't pay much attention to to the stylization or rules of formal artistic theory. Instead, the artist focuses on the accurate depiction of life forms and objects, perspective, good composition, lights and darks, and ciolor and tone.
Photorealism (also known as hyper realism)
- is an art style where the artwork appears as if it's a photo. This art style keeps track of every detail of the subject from a single grain to a pore. In a photorealism art, nothing is left out.
Painterly
- the art style where the strokes of the brush is visible and the colors not thoroughly blended.
Impressionism
- the art style where only small strokes of the brush is visble.
Abstract Art (also known as contemporary art)
- an art that doesn't resemble anything from "real life". The artist tends to use shapes and colors to create their art and the meaning behind the art usually depends on how a viewer depicts it.
Surrealism
- a modern art style of painting that juxtaposes, various abstract concepts together to give a startling effect. It is characterized by fully recognizable images which are realistically painted, taken out of their normal setting and contexts then reassembled or organized within an ambiguous, paradoxical, or shocking framework.
Pop Art
- an art style where the artist use bright (usually neon colors) to captivate the viewer's eye. This art started in 1950 to promote many things such as products and celebrities.

What is Drawing?

Drawing is a form of visual art in which one uses various drawing instruments to mark paper or another two-dimensional medium. Instruments include graphite pencils, pen and ink, various kinds of paints, inked brushes, colored pencils, crayons, charcoal, chalk, pastels, various kinds of erasers, markers, styluses, and various metals (such as silverpoint). Digital drawing is the act of using a computer to draw. Common methods of digital drawing include a stylus or finger on a touchscreen device, stylus- or finger-to-touchpad, or in some cases, a mouse. There are many digital art programs and devices.

Just like Painting, drawing also has other styles or topics that falls underneath the. The list below is the list for what these styles are. However, this list is incomplete since some styles are considered as architectural styles so those were not included.

Illustration Drawing
- drawing in order to document; drawing to clearly state and render intent, style, size, color, character, effect, and so on. More on Illustration.
Life Drawing
- drawing as a means of expression; drawing from direct observation, as in still-life or figure drawing.
Emotive Drawing
- drawing, like painting, as an expressive way to explore and put forth feeling, mood, self, time, and so on; drawing as a sensitive expression of personality. More on Emotive Drawing.
Geometric Drawing
- drawing as a means to precisely represent all aspects of construction; drawing that shows measured scale, true sides, sections, and a variety of descriptive views.
