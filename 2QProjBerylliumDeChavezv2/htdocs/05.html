Music

What is Music?

Music is a unique and beautiful art form. It is a free way of expressing one's feelings through patterned construction of words, melody, rhythm, and harmony of instruments. It is an art form concerned with combing vocal or instrumental sounds to create the music we know. It need not to always have vocals, there are also music that are completely instrumental.

Among the numerous other fine arts, music ends up being perhaps the most unmistakable in view of its capacity to take the state of a huge number of various things. Regardless of whether a specific tune or artist addresses a memory, a feeling, a huge life occasion, or even only a specific mind-set, music as a craftsmanship allots us with the chance to feel and communicate in a more unpredictable and important manner.

What about the history of Music?

Music has evolved along with the inventions of humans through the years. Almost every decade, there is a new genre. Even the melodies has evolvesd. the way of singing and choice of instruments as well. But what about the past? How did music start?

Dating back to early men, it has been discovered that very early man developed primitive flutes from animal bones and stones and wood as percussion. Then comes voice, they used their voices to bond socially or comfort a child who can't sleep. The history of music is a long interesting one.

More on the History of Music

There are 51 genres of music and all are pleasant to listen to, well depending on the person. However, since 51 genres are too much, the list below are only the top music genres in the world.

Classical
- art music produced or rooted in the traditions of Western culture, including both liturgical (religious) and secular music.
Country
- a genre of popular music that originated with blues, old-time music, and various types of American folk music including Appalachian, Cajun, and the cowboy Western music styles of New Mexico, Red Dirt, Tejano, and Texas country.
Electronic dance music (EDM)
- encompasses styles ranging from beatless ambient music to 200-beats-per-minute hardcore, with house music, techno, drum and bass, dubstep, and trance among the most-notable examples.
Hip-hop
- a style of popular music of US black and Hispanic origin, featuring rap with an electronic backing.
Indie rock
- characterized by the use of unique or less-popular instruments.
Jazz
- is a broad style of music characterized by complex harmony, syncopated rhythms, and a heavy emphasis on improvisation.
Metal
- a loud, aggressive style of rock music.
Oldies
- is a term for musical genres such as pop music, rock and roll, doo-wop, surf music (broadly characterized as classic rock and pop rock) from the second half of the 20th century, specifically from around the mid-1950s to the 1980s.
Pop
- is a genre of popular music that originated in its modern form during the mid-1950s in the United States and the United Kingdom.
Rhythm & blues (R&B)
- a genre of popular music that originated in African-American communities in the 1940s.
Rock
- a broad genre of popular music that originated as "rock and roll" in the United States in the late 1940s and early 1950s, developing into a range of different styles in the mid-1960s and later, particularly in the United States and the United Kingdom.

Music is produced with the help of its elements. Music won't be considered music without the aid of its elements as there is a chance that it'd only be pure chaos and clashing of sounds produced by the instruments. The list below shows the basic elements of music needed to produce a song or a piece pleasabnt to human ears.

Pitch
- position of a solitary sound in the total scope of sound. Sounds are higher or lower in pitch as per the recurrence of vibration of the sound waves creating them.
Duration
- length of time that a tone persists.
Dynamics
- how quietly or loudly a piece of music is played. Dynamics also helps to convey the mood or emotion of the melody.
Tempo
- speed of the piece or song.
Timbre
- the perceived sound quality of a musical note, sound, or tone.
Texture
- how the tempo, melodic, and harmonic materials are combined in a musical composition, determining the overall quality of the sound in a piece.
Structure
- structure of a musical composition or performance.
